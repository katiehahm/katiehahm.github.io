µTugs for Macro Applications

These “µTugs” are micro robots with gecko adhesives that can pull up to 2000 times their weight! During the summer of 2015, I worked in the Biomimetics and Dexterous Manipulation Laboratory at Stanford University under Prof. Mark Cutkosky on manufacturing 7 µTugs to pull very large loads.

I also researched the general principles of load sharing for various small robots (including the µTug) by studying their gait. I conducted experiments, built experimental setups and used MATLAB to process extensive data on these tests.
