Making, Breaking, and Analyzing Bridges

In our solid mechanics class, we designed and built a small bride out of balsa wood. During the design process, we calculated the compression and tension values within the bridge to make sure our design was optimized. The image above is the final drawing of our bridge with the calculated values, and from this model we built the bridge shown on the left.

After making the bridge, we tested it until failure by putting a weight in the middle of the bridge, then increasing the weight until it broke. The breaking weight was as expected from our analysis.
